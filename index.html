The Programs

There are 5 different types of exchange program all centred around assimilating the host countryâ€™s culture with a focus on learning the language. All of these programs start
with one month of intensive language learning classes of at fifteen hours per week, called the initiation stage. Intensive lessons are then continued throughout the stay but are reduced to eight hours per week. Then the programs differ:

  • HIGH-SCHOOLERS: For high-schoolers there is only one program, which is completing a full school year in a high-school in the host country. They will stay with a host family and join in with their day to day lives to be fully immersed in their culture.
    • Read more
  • JUST OUT OF HIGH-SCHOOL: For young adults just out of high school, there are two programs. The first, for those who do not wish to continue with tertiary education, will be inducted into an apprenticeship for a trade such as mechanics or the culinary arts. These young adults have the option to stay with a family or in a share-house with their peers from the host country.

    The second, for those wishing to continue tertiary education, will attend a local university, taking classes from a Kiwi University, as well as up to two classes in the local university. They will have the same options for accommodation as the first program.
    • Read more
  • FINAL YEAR OF UNIVERSITY: For students in the final year of their degree, there are two programs.The first, is to complete papers from their Kiwi University as well as up to two papers from a local university.

    The second is a paid internship in their respective industry, as well as finishing their papers from their Kiwi University. These students will be staying in a share-house with their peers from the host country.
